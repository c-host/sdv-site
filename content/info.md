**Stacey de Voe** is a visual artist (b.1988) in New York and currently based in Malmö, Sweden. Through a research oriented approach, her practice uses site specificity and the archive to investigate broader issues concerning material histories, collective memory, feminized labor and friendship. She stages and modulates historiography through a relational process, forging an intimacy with the places and people she inquires.

de Voe's practice is not bound to a specific medium yet utilizes installation, moving image, performance, printmaking and publication often combining artisanal and mass-produced objects, archival material, personal accounts and fiction to create new narratives. These material assemblages give way to new understandings and perspectives surrounding the social, economical and political crises of our time. Her practice unfolds through long-term thematic investigations where exhibitions, publications and performance based work can coexist and converge within the same thematic framework. Embracing this way of working allows her to take detours, often revealing fragments of history that have at one point or another sought to be concealed. Through both magnification and abstraction new interpretations are allowed to emerge. Enlarging the understanding and complexity of the histories she is drawn towards.

For the last few years de Voe's practice has revolved around the material histories of thread and the defunct textile industries of Europe. Through several projects and exhibitions she has scrutinized the inherent effects that this once blooming, now bygone industry has had on welfare politics along with its close, yet silent ties to the colonial system through the buying and trading of raw materials and finished goods. Her relationship with textiles is prompted by her own experiences working within the garment industries of New York and Paris, as well as her matriarchal lineage of immigrant furriers and hosiery sellers.
