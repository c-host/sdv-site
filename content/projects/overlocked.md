**overlocked** is an ongoing artistic research project which seeks to unravel the historical intricacies surrounding the production of counterfeit silk, commonly known as nylon and its contemporary implications on femininity, labor and representation. The project maps the history of material and bodily processes with their connections to social and infrastructural conditions.

Through a deep investigation of architectures of consumer capitalism, the department store, and forgotten sites of labor, the factory, the project provokes a critical reflection around the elastic imaginaries promoted by this synthetic thread. Through strenuous research surrounding Swedish welfare politics the project makes several links between the influence of privatization around the rhetoric of accessibility and democracy. The importing of nylon thread and its onset into the Swedish market not only influenced the economy but also had a lasting effect on society.

Thus far, the project has manifested through various stagings. The first was a large-scale solo exhibition in the spring of 2023 at gallery KHM1 in Malmö. In this context, the research took form through a 3-channel video and 6-channel audio installation. The second iteration of this research was executed in collaboration with AMIFF-International Film Festival in Harstad, Norway. For this occasion I condensed the multi-channel work into a single essay film. It had its premiere at the film festival in October 2023 with the title overlocked- A Round in Three Threads. In 2026 I presented the project in a group exhibition as a series of sculptural assemblages utilizing tactile knowledge inherited from the artist's mother and notions of display such as packaging and podiums often seen in department stores and other cathedrals of consumption. Through sculptural assemblage, enlarging, obscuring and displacing seemingly unrelated objects and historical residues the works resist commodification and instead point to the ways that materials and bodies manage to exceed and even disrupt the systems that attempt to contain them.

Through a visual juxtaposition of personal accounts and archival findings overlocked seeks to connect biographical aspects, fragmented chronologies, memory and historical events with our present condition. Narratives where political, historical, and critical insights are mixed with facts, fiction, and storytelling.
